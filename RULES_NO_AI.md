# 🚫 THE NO-AI RULES (Read and Mean It)

This course only works if you obey one rule: **do not use AI to think for you.** Here's exactly what that means.

---

## ❌ NOT ALLOWED (this is cheating yourself)
- ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any chatbot to write or explain your exercises
- GitHub Copilot or any AI autocomplete in your editor (turn it OFF)
- "Just asking AI" the moment you feel stuck
- Copy-pasting solutions from anywhere

## ✅ ALLOWED (this is real learning)
- **Official Python documentation:** https://docs.python.org/3/
- **Your own notes** and this course's guides
- **A search engine** to look up *what a function does* — but NOT to find the full answer to your exercise
- **Your own brain, paper, and pen** (your best tools)
- Reading error messages slowly and figuring them out yourself

---

## Why so strict?

When AI writes your code, **you feel productive but learn nothing.** Your problem-solving muscle only grows when *you* do the reps. It's like watching someone lift weights — you don't get stronger. The discomfort of being stuck is the exact moment learning happens. Don't rob yourself of it.

## When you get stuck (and you will)

Follow this **15-minute rule** instead of reaching for AI:
1. **Re-read the problem.** What is it *actually* asking? (2 min)
2. **Write the steps in plain words** (your own language is fine), not code. (3 min)
3. **Check the Python docs** for the specific tool you need. (3 min)
4. **Try something** — even a wrong attempt teaches you. (5 min)
5. **Take a 2-minute break**, then look again with fresh eyes.

If after all that you're still stuck, write down *exactly* what's confusing you in your daily log. Move on, sleep, and try again tomorrow. Stuck-then-solved is how you get strong.

## The mindset

> "I would rather write 5 ugly lines that are MINE than 50 perfect lines that are the AI's."

Ugly, working, self-written code on Day 10 beats perfect AI code every time — because the ugly code means *your brain did it.* That brain is what gets you hired and keeps you employed.
